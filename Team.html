Most industrial processes rely on the measurement of pressure; therefore pressure calibration plays a major part in a companyâ€™s quality assurance. Even the highest quality instruments are subject to drift over time, resulting in inaccurate measurements and substandard performance. It is very important that all instruments are calibrated by trained, competent and approved personnel to maintain instrument integrity.

     

Neglecting calibration can result in downtime, inferior products or process quality issues. Instruments that are critical to the process can drift out of specification and this is a risk to personnel and equipment safety. The cost of calibration is usually lower as compared to production down time or injury to personnel.
